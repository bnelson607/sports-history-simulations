Ranking the Most Difficult Super Bowl Paths

Winning the Super Bowl is hard. But not all Super Bowl runs are created equal. Some champions had to claw their way through elite opponents and survive road games in hostile environments. Others benefited from home field advantage and earlier upsets that took out their biggest competitors.

There are two ways to define a “difficult” playoff path. One focuses on the strength of the teams a contender faced using their average opponent Elo rating – it doesn’t matter how strong or weak the eventual champion was. The second method assesses how likely it was for that team to win each game based on win probabilities. This reflects how impressive a run was by highlighting teams that overcame longer odds. The teams that appear here had a more difficult path because they were less likely to win each game – even if its opponents weren’t elite by Elo standards – due to being a weaker team or having to play on the road.

This analysis covers identifies the hardest paths to a Super Bowl title by examining the caliber of opponents the eventual champion faced, measured by their opponents’ Elo ratings. We limit the analysis to Super Bowls since 1970, guaranteeing that every champion faced a modern playoff structure that required them to win at least three games to claim the Lombardi trophy.

  1. 2001 New England Patriots – 1717.37 Opponent Elo
    • Regular Season Summary
      • Starting Elo: 1468 (25th of 31 teams)
      • Record: 11-5, 1st in AFC East, 2-seed
      • Ending Elo: 1639 (7th of 31 teams) | +171 (4th of 31 teams)
      • Defining Fact: Rebounded from an 0-2 start to win 11 of the last 14 games, including the final 6, after Brady replaced Bledsoe in Week 3; final loss came in Week 10 against the Rams
    • Playoff Summary
      • Wild Card Round: Bye
      • Divisional Round: vs. Oakland (10-6, 3-seed, 1624 Elo)
        • Result: Won 16-13
        • Postgame Elo: 1653 (+14)
        • Defining Fact: Game played in heavy snow; best known for the “Tuck Rule” incident that allowed the Patriots to tie the game at 13 before winning in overtime
      • Conference Championship: @ Pittsburgh (13-3, 1-seed, 1722 Elo)
        • Result: Won 24-17
        • Postgame Elo: 1685 (+22)
        • Defining Fact: Forced two 4th quarter interceptions in the last 3 minutes of the game to protect a 24-17 lead
      • Super Bowl XXXVI: vs. St. Louis Rams (14-2, 1-seed, 1806 Elo)
        • Result: Won 20-17
        • Postgame Elo: 1706 (+21)
        • Defining Fact: Forced three turnovers and held “The Greatest Show on Turf” that averaged 31.4 ppg to their second lowest point total of the season; considered one of the biggest upsets in Super Bowl history
    • What If? The Patriots won 3 one-possession games against division rivals in their last 5 games. A single loss would have relegated them to a wild-card spot instead of a first-round bye, making their Super Bowl path longer and more challenging.
