Ranking the Most Difficult Super Bowl Paths: Part 2

The previous article highlighted the five most difficult paths to winning a Super Bowl based on their opponent’s average Elo ratings. This analysis will look at the winners from a different perspective – how likely it was that the eventual champion would survive their journey to the Lombardi Trophy based on the likelihood of winning each game. As before, only teams that played at least 3 playoffs games were considered.

  1. 1997 Denver Broncos – 4.82% Probability of Survival
    • Regular Season Summary
      • Starting Elo: 1549 (5th of 30 teams)
      • Record: 12-4, 2nd in AFC West, 4-seed
      • Ending Elo: 1663 (3rd of 30 teams) | +114 (4th of 30 teams)
      • Defining Fact: Had the highest scoring offense (472), the highest point differential (+185), and were the last remaining undefeated team (6-0), but had to settle for a wild card spot after losing the division to the Chiefs by 1 game
    • Playoff Summary
      • Wild Card Round: vs. Jacksonville (11-5, 68.89%)
        • Result: Won 42-17
        • Postgame Elo: 1692 (+29)
        • Defining Fact: Avenged their upset loss to the Jaguars in the 1996 Divisional Round by holding the ball for over 40 minutes, gaining 511 total yards, and putting the game away with 21 4th quarter points
      • Divisional Round: @ Kansas City (13-3, 36.18%)
        • Result: Won 14-10
        • Postgame Elo: 1716 (+24)
        • Defining Fact: Won on the road despite being outgained (303-272) and losing the turnover battle (2-0) as the top seed in the AFC would lose in the Divisional Round for the third straight year
      • Conference Championship: @ Pittsburgh (11-5, 52.29%)
        • Result: Won 24-21
        • Postgame Elo: 1732 (+16)
        • Defining Fact: Scored all 24 points in the first half and relied on their defense by forcing 4 turnovers and holding Pittsburgh to 7 second half points
      • Super Bowl XXXII: vs. Green Bay (13-3, 37.00%)
        • Result: Won 31-24
        • Postgame Elo: 1763 (+31)
        • Defining Fact: Entered the game as 11-point underdogs to the defending champions, but only trailed for one drive after Green Bay’s touchdown to open the game; first Super Bowl victory for an AFC team since the 1983 season
    • Aftermath: Entered the 1998 season as one of the favorites to repeat, begin the season 13-0, and become one of three 14-win teams in the NFL en route to repeating as Super Bowl champions
  2. 2023 Kansas City Chiefs – 2.93% Probability of Survival
    • Regular Season Summary
      • Starting Elo: 1620 (1st of 32 teams)
      • Record: 11-6, 1st in AFC West, 3-seed
      • Ending Elo: 1608 (1st of 32 teams) | -12 (19th of 32 teams)
      • Defining Fact: Won fewer than 12 games and scored fewer than 450 points for the first time since 2017, and lost more than 3 games at home for the first time under Andy Reid’s tenure, but were carried by the league’s second-best scoring defense
    • Playoff Summary
      • Wild Card Round: vs. Miami (11-6, 60.39%)
        • Result: Won 26-7
        • Postgame Elo: 1634 (+26)
        • Defining Fact: Held the league’s second-highest scoring offense (29.2 ppg) to a season-low 7 points in subzero temperatures
      • Divisional Round: @ Buffalo (11-6, 35.22%)
        • Result: Won 27-24
        • Postgame Elo: 1650 (+16)
        • Defining Fact: Buffalo dominated time of possession (37:03–22:57) in a game with 5 lead changes and only a missed field goal preventing overtime
      • Conference Championship: @ Baltimore (13-4, 29.23%)
        • Result: Won 17-10
        • Postgame Elo: 1677 (+27)
        • Defining Fact: Defense shut down the 4th highest scoring offense in the league (28.4 ppg) and forced 3 turnovers while holding the ball for over 37 minutes
      • Super Bowl LVIII: vs. San Francisco (12-5, 47.15%)
        • Result: Won 25-22
        • Postgame Elo: 1691 (+14)
        • Defining Fact: Mahomes orchestrated his 4th double-digit playoff comeback after being down 10-0 late in the first half; a late 4th quarter drive tied the game at 19 to set up overtime in the longest Super Bowl in history
    • Aftermath: 2024 Chiefs finished the regular season 15-2 but went 11-0 in one-possession games. They returned to the Super Bowl but were dominated by the Eagles in a game that wasn’t as close as the 40-22 score indicated
  3. 2012 Baltimore Ravens – 2.80% Probability of Survival
    • Regular Season Summary
      • Starting Elo: 1573 (6th of 32 teams)
      • Record: 10-6, 1st in AFC North, 4-seed
      • Ending Elo: 1598 (8th of 32 teams) | +25 (16th of 32 teams)
      • Defining Fact: Were in contention for the 1-seed after starting 9-2, but dropped 4 of their final 5 to fall to the 4-seed and tie for the worst record among playoff teams
    • Playoff Summary
      • Wild Card Round: vs. Indianapolis (11-5, 70.66%)
        • Result: Won 24-9
        • Postgame Elo: 1615 (+17)
        • Defining Fact: Gave up 419 total yards but held the Colts to just 3 field goals
      • Divisional Round: @ Denver (13-3, 32.81%)
        • Result: Won 38-35
        • Postgame Elo: 1633 (+18)
        • Defining Fact: Flacco’s 70-yard TD pass with 31 seconds left forced OT; Ravens outlasted 9-point favorites
      • Conference Championship: @ New England (12-4, 26.63%)
        • Result: Won 28-13
        • Postgame Elo: 1676 (+43)
        • Defining Fact: Shut out Patriots in 2nd half and forced 3 turnovers
      • Super Bowl XLVII: vs. San Francisco (11-4-1, 45.39%)
        • Result: Won 34-31
        • Postgame Elo: 1691 (+15)
        • Defining Fact: Led 28-6 before a blackout; held off furious comeback
    • Aftermath: Missed the playoffs in 2013 after finishing 8-8 following a Week 17 loss to Cincinnati
  4. 2011 New York Giants – 1.10% Probability of Survival
    • Regular Season Summary
      • Starting Elo: 1524 (12th of 32 teams)
      • Record: 9-7, 1st in NFC East, 4-seed
      • Ending Elo: 1533 (12th of 32 teams) | +9 (17th of 32 teams)
      • Defining Fact: Outscored during the season, lost 5 of 6 midseason games, but beat Dallas in Week 17 to win division
    • Playoff Summary
      • Wild Card Round: vs. Atlanta (10-6, 49.23%)
        • Result: Won 24-2
        • Postgame Elo: 1576 (+43)
        • Defining Fact: Held Falcons to 247 yards; only Atlanta score was a safety
      • Divisional Round: @ Green Bay (15-1, 19.15%)
        • Result: Won 37-20
        • Postgame Elo: 1637 (+61)
        • Defining Fact: Never trailed, forced 4 turnovers, and beat top-seeded Packers at Lambeau
      • Conference Championship: @ San Francisco (13-3, 35.43%)
        • Result: Won 20-17 (OT)
        • Postgame Elo: 1657 (+20)
        • Defining Fact: Held 49ers to 1-for-13 on 3rd down and won in overtime
      • Super Bowl XLVI: vs. New England (13-3, 33.08%)
        • Result: Won 21-17
        • Postgame Elo: 1681 (+24)
        • Defining Fact: Won on late TD pass; became worst regular season team (9-7) to win a Super Bowl
    • Aftermath: Went 9-7 again in 2012, but missed the playoffs despite a +85 point differential
  5. 2007 New York Giants – 0.87% Probability of Survival
    • Regular Season Summary
      • Starting Elo: 1495 (18th of 32 teams)
      • Record: 10-6, 2nd in NFC East, 5-seed
      • Ending Elo: 1537 (12th of 32 teams) | +42 (15th of 32 teams)
      • Defining Fact: A Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde type of team, the Giants (1) surrendered 80 points during an 0-2 start while dropping to a 1428 Elo rating, (2) won 6 games in a row, holding 5 of their opponents to 17 points or fewer, and (3) went 4-4 in the second half with the season finale being a close 3-point loss to the undefeated Patriots
    • Playoff Summary
      • Wild Card Round: @ Tampa Bay (9-7, 44.63%)
        • Result: Won 24-14
        • Postgame Elo: 1567 (+30)
        • Defining Fact: A rather uneventful game in which the Giants forced 3 turnovers and scored 24 points between two Buccaneers touchdowns
      • Divisional Round: @ Dallas (13-3, 31.90%)
        • Result: Won 21-17
        • Postgame Elo: 1591 (+24)
        • Defining Fact: Outgained 336-230 and out-possessed by 36:30 minutes, the Giants needed an interception in the end zone with 9 seconds remaining to preserve the win and knock the NFC top-seed out of the playoffs in the Divisional Round for the first time since 1987
      • Conference Championship: @ Green Bay (13-3, 34.04%)
        • Result: Won 23-20 (OT)
        • Postgame Elo: 1610 (+19)
        • Defining Fact: Held the Packers to 264 total yards, and survived 4 lead changes and subzero temperatures at Lambeau in overtime in a game that neither team led by more than 6 points
      • Super Bowl XLII: vs. New England (16-0, 17.85%)
        • Result: Won 17-14
        • Postgame Elo: 1635 (+25)
        • Defining Fact: Entered the game as 12.5-point underdogs, held a Patriots offense averaging nearly 37 ppg to 14 points and 274 total yards while using a touchdown with 35 seconds remaining to pull off the biggest upset in Super Bowl history
    • Aftermath: The 2008 Giants started strong, winning 11 of their first 12 games, but faltered late and lost 3 of their final 4. Regardless, the Giants earned the 1-seed in the NFC, but were upset in the Divisional Round by the Eagles 23-11.
